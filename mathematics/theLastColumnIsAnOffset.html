Don't call the last column a multiplier

This is just a small rant about a matrix misnomer I came across. For the sake discretion, everything considered is hypothetical.

During the course of reading tutorials on SVG filters, eventually you'll start reading up on <feColorMatrix> and depending on the source of the article you choose to read they might have an illustration of the transformation matrix that you feed <feColorMatrix>. It might look something like this.

[ R G B A M r 11 r 12 r 13 r 14 r 15 g 11 g 12 g 13 g 14 g 15 b 11 b 12 b 13 b 14 b 15 a 11 a 12 a 13 a 14 a 15 ]

With the R, G, B, A and M taken to stand for red, green, blue, alpha and multiplier respectively. If you are at all familiar with matrix arithmetic that last label should bother you. Consider the full equation of the transformation that <feColorMatrix> applies.

[ R′ G′ B′ A′ 1 ] = [ r 11 r 12 r 13 r 14 r 15 g 11 g 12 g 13 g 14 g 15 b 11 b 12 b 13 b 14 b 15 a 11 a 12 a 13 a 14 a 15 0 0 0 0 1 ] [ R G B A 1 ]

While the column labeled multiplier does indeed get multipled by 1. The entries themself are not multiplied with the original color; they simply contribute a fixed amount to the final amount to the final pixel color. Instead of calling that last column a "multiplier", call it an offset because that is what it does.
