A Geometric Geometric series.

When initially introduced to geometric series your first thought might be "where's the geometry?". Arithmetic series arithmetic kind of makes sense, addition is part of arithmetic. But geometric?

The series Σ n = 0 ∞ x n = 1 + x + x 2 + ... is convergent if the common ratio | x | < 1 . Is there a function with a strong connection to geometry has a range that falls within this interval?

The case 0<x<1

Consider the figure above that is easily constructed with compass and straigtedge. Start with a horizontal line. Upon this construct a right angled triangle with a hypotenuse of length 1. The base of the triangle is then cos Θ . Taking this as the length for the hypotenuse of a second triangle with a base of length cos 2 Θ . A line drawn through the vertices of the triangles intersects the horizontal line at P. With this line a triangle with a base of length 1 can be constructed to the left of the first.

The sum of the triangle's bases is then Σ n = 0 ∞ cos n Θ = 1 + cos Θ + cos 2 Θ + ... a geometric series with common ratio cos Θ
