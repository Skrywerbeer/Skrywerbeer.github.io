Paper Demonstrations

Showing what you wrote

Paper, besides being a medium for writing mathematics on, can be used to do mathematics. If you discount the inaccuracies of the real world a sheet of paper is a plane that you can hold in your hands. Through folding this plane certain constructions can be done much faster than with a ruler and compass.

Bisection

The middle of a line segment is that point of the segment that divides it into two parts of equal length. How can you check if the two lengths are the same? Two line segments have the same length if their end points, possiblly through a rotation of one of the segments, can be made to conincide. And if you fold something in two you automatically have two segements with coinciding endpoints. The same thing works on paper. Any line segment on a sheet can be bisected just by bringing the ends together not just the edges of the sheet.

Angles can be bisected by a similar argument. By folding the two lines that define the angle so that they conincide at everypoint, the crease forms such that the two lines diverge from the crease at equal rates. The crease is then that line that bisects the angle.
